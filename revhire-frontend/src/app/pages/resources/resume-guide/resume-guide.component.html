Resume Building Guide

Craft a resume that gets you noticed.

1. Tailor for the Job

Your resume should not be a one-size-fits-all document. Look closely at the job description of the role you are applying for, and ensure your resume mirrors the keywords and phrases used by the employer.

2. Keep it Concise

Recruiters spend an average of 6 seconds reviewing a resume. Keep your formatting clean, your bullet points short, and ideally limit the length to a single page if you have less than 10 years of experience.

3. Emphasize Achievements

Instead of simply listing your duties, outline what you achieved. Use the XYZ formula: "Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]." For example, "Increased sales by 20% in 6 months by implementing a new CRM system."

4. Formatting Matters

Use standard, professional fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Inter. Ensure your contact information is easily visible at the top, and apply consistent margins and spacing throughout the document to ensure ATS (Applicant Tracking System) readability.
