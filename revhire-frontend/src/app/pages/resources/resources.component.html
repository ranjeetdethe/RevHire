Resume Building

Your resume is your first impression. Make it count by ensuring it is highly readable and targeted precisely at the roles you want.

  • Tailor your resume to the specific job description by mirroring key phrases.
  • Highlight quantifiable achievements (e.g., "Increased sales by 20%").
  • Keep it concise—ideally one page for early-career professionals.
  • Use standard, clean fonts (Arial, ATS-friendly) and a simple layout.

Interview Prep

An interview is a two-way conversation. Confidence comes from preparation and understanding the core values of the business.

  • Research the company culture, mission statement, and recent news.
  • Master the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for behavioral questions.
  • Prepare 2-3 insightful questions to ask the interviewer.
  • Test your tech setup and background if interviewing remotely.

Salary Negotiation

Don't leave money on the table. Negotiating your total compensation package is a standard and expected professional practice.

  • Research valid industry benchmarks using Glassdoor or PayScale.
  • Avoid being the first to throw out an absolute number if possible.
  • Factor in the total compensation package (equity, bonuses, PTO).
  • Always remain professional, polite, and confident in your market value.
