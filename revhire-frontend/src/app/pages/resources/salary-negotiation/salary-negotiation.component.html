Salary Negotiation 101

Ensure you're getting exactly what you're worth.

1. Don't Throw The First Number

A fundamental rule in negotiation is allowing the company to set the anchor price. Wait until you have a formal offer on the table before negotiating, or ask the recruiter for the typical "salary band" designated for that specific role.

2. Understand Your Market Value

Never negotiate completely blind. Use resources like Glassdoor, PayScale, and Levels.fyi to research what the standard industry pay is specifically for someone in your exact city with your exact years of experience, holding that precise job title.

3. Analyze Total Compensation

Money isn't just base salary. A lower base might be totally justifiable if the employer offers incredibly robust stock options, equity, quarterly performance bonuses, extensive PTO, sign-on bonuses, or 401(k) matching. Always consider the full package.

4. Maintain Professionalism

Remember that you are speaking to future colleagues. Negotiation is a discussion, not a fight. Validate their initial offer with gratitude ("I'm thrilled about this offer and excited to join"), then transition smoothly into your value-based counter-offer ("However, based on my skill XYZ and the current market rates, I'd like to explore...").
